Tree planting in Testour
In Testour, the tree planting project focuses on the olive tree, an evergreen plant known for its active role in carbon dioxide sequestration. The olive tree's deep roots help stabilize the soil, reducing erosion risk and enhancing the health of local ecosystems. Well-managed olive groves can also act as carbon sinks, storing carbon in the soil. Therefore, incorporating olive trees into climate change strategies presents a comprehensive approach that merges environmental preservation with agricultural production and carbon sequestration, paving the way for a more sustainable future. Additionally, the almond tree, while its roots are not as deep as those of the olive tree, offers similar environmental benefits.â€‹

The Testour region:
The tree planting project is situated in the Testour region of Tunisia, an area distinguished by its diverse and functional natural landscape. Dominated by expansive olive groves, the region forms a robust agricultural base. The gently rolling hills provide panoramic views of these plantations, and the rivers weaving through the area support the irrigation essential for local agriculture. The inhabitants of Testour benefit from this agricultural richness, with the surrounding mountains offering a picturesque natural backdrop.
