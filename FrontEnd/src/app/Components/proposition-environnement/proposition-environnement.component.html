Environment

Strategies for reducing greenhouse gas emissions
  • Conduct a comprehensive and accurate assessment of the company's GHG emissions to identify primary sources and opportunities for reduction.
  • Implement energy-saving technologies, adopt energy management practices, and renovate buildings for increased energy efficiency.
  • Recycle electronic equipment responsibly through certified recycling programs, recovering valuable materials and minimizing environmental impacts.
  • Invest in carbon sequestration or carbon offset projects to compensate for emissions that cannot be directly reduced.
  • Foster employee involvement in the emissions reduction strategy by increasing their awareness of climate issues and encouraging active participation.
Waste management and recycling practices
  • Establish a waste sorting system within your company.​
  • If your business generates organic waste (e.g., food scraps or garden waste), consider implementing a composting system.
  • Invest in renewable energy sources, such as solar, wind, and hydroelectric, to reduce reliance on fossil fuels.
  • Identify and reduce the use of single-use items in daily operations.​
  • Opt for biodegradable and compostable packaging materials for shipping products or packing goods.
Valorization of renewable energy
  • Assess the feasibility of installing solar panels on rooftops or company grounds.​
  • Consider cogeneration systems for simultaneous production of heat and electricity from renewable sources.​
  • Replace traditional light bulbs with energy-efficient LED bulbs.​
  • Aim to acquire recognized environmental certifications, like ISO 14001.​
  • Investigate investment opportunities in external renewable energy projects.​
Water management strategies
  • Regularly monitor and inspect installations and swiftly repair any detected leaks.​
  • Select water-efficient sanitary equipment and appliances.​
  • Educate and raise awareness among employees about water conservation.​
  • Conduct a comprehensive water footprint assessment to understand the impact of your operations on water resources.
  • Implement a rainwater harvesting system for non-potable uses like irrigation or washing exterior surfaces.
Actions for the preservation of biodiversity and the environment
  • Develop green spaces on your business site, preserving existing vegetation and promoting native species planting.
  • Promote sustainable transportation options among employees, such as carpooling, cycling, public transport, or teleworking.
  • Evaluate and minimize the use of potentially harmful chemicals in your operations.​
  • Participate in carbon offset programs to neutralize your company's GHG emissions.​
  • Conduct life cycle analyses of products to assess environmental impacts throughout their lifespan.
