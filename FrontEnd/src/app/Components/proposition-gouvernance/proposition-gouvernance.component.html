  • Set up an independent audit committee within the board to oversee financial matters, internal controls, and regulatory compliance.
  • Implement a conflict of interest disclosure policy for board members, executives, and key employees.

Transparency and Integrity of the Company
  • Develop and communicate a policy of transparency that encourages openness and the disclosure of relevant information to stakeholders.
  • Set up confidential reporting mechanisms to enable employees to report illegal practices, wrongful acts, or ethical violations without fear of retaliation.
  • Establish robust internal controls to prevent fraudulent or dishonest practices.
  • Respect the confidentiality and protection of personal data of clients, employees, and other stakeholders.
  • Formulate a clear and strong anti-corruption policy that prohibits all forms of corruption, including bribes, kickbacks, and unfair business practices.
Conflict of Interest Prevention and Responsible Management
  • Implement a clear separation of roles to avoid potential conflicts of interest.
  • Ensure that all company contracts and transactions are transparent and fair.
  • Establish a clear policy regarding gifts, invitations, and hospitality offered to or received by company employees (Set reasonable limits and define rules to prevent conflict of interest situations or favoritism).
  • Strengthen internal controls to detect and prevent fraudulent behavior, embezzlement, and abuse.
  • Establish transparent and honest relationships with all stakeholders, including customers, suppliers, investors, and civil society.
Encouragement of Business Ethics and Responsibility
  • Create an environment where employees feel comfortable reporting ethical issues or asking questions without fear of retaliation.
  • Implement reward and recognition mechanisms for employees who demonstrate exemplary ethical behavior and business responsibility.
  • Integrate social and environmental responsibility into the company's strategy and operations.
  • Ensure company leaders serve as models of ethics and responsibility, as their behavior can profoundly influence the company's culture.
  • Encourage employees to share their ideas and concerns, which can help identify potential ethical issues before they escalate into serious problems.
Stakeholder Involvement in Decision-Making Processes
